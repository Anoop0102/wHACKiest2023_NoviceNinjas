Fitness tips!

Stand Up for Your Health


Sitting is the new smoking!

Make a point to stand up and move around for at least 2-3 minutes every hour.

Quick tips: Set a reminder on your phone or computer to stand up and move around every hour. Use a standing or treadmill desk to encourage movement throughout the day.

Take short breaks to walk around the office or outside during lunch.

Incorporate stretching or light exercises into your work routine.

Make a habit of standing up and walking during phone calls.

Use a pedometer or fitness tracker to monitor your daily steps and aim to increase them.

Keep a water bottle nearby and refill it frequently to remind you to stand up.

Deskercise


No gym membership required

ncorporate small exercises into your workday, such as stretching, shoulder rolls, and desk chair dips. These exercises can help to alleviate tension and improve posture.

Stay Hydrated


Drink up to stay on top

Drink water regularly throughout the day: Set a reminder to drink water every hour or so, and make sure to drink a glass or two with each meal.

Carry a water bottle with you: Keep a refillable water bottle with you at all times, whether you're at work, running errands, or working out.

Eat water-rich foods: Fruits and vegetables like cucumbers, watermelon, and strawberries are high in water content and can help keep you hydrated.

Limit diuretics: Avoid beverages that can dehydrate you, such as alcohol and caffeine.

Check your urine color: If your urine is pale yellow, it's a sign that you're well-hydrated. If it's dark yellow or amber, you may need to drink more water.

Take a Break


Because your brain needs it

Taking short breaks throughout the day can help to reduce stress, improve focus, and boost productivity. Use this time to walk around, take a quick stretch, or simply look away from your computer screen

Eat Right


Fuel your body for the work ahead

Eating a healthy diet can help to boost energy levels, improve mood, and prevent weight gain. Avoid processed foods, sugary snacks, and excessive caffeine. Instead, opt for fruits, vegetables, and lean proteins.

Good Posture


Sit up and take notice

Maintaining good posture can help to prevent back pain, headaches, and muscle strain. Make sure your chair is adjusted to the right height, your computer screen is at eye level, and your feet are flat on the ground.

Stay Active


Physical activity is non-negotiable

Regular exercise can help to improve overall health, reduce stress, and boost energy levels. Make a point to get at least 30 minutes of physical activity each day, whether it's going for a walk, hitting the gym, or taking a yoga class.
